# Book 6: The Frightened Fawn
## A Whisperwood Story

*Resources: Antler vibrations · Owls · Bunnies · Evacuation routes | Theme: The sensor can't sense if it's not rested.*

---

### Chapter 1: Dallas at the Edge

It was an ordinary morning in Whisperwood.

Dallas the deer stood at the edge of the tremor meadow with her antlers raised toward the sky. She did this every day. She stood there, and she listened, and she felt the vibrations that moved through the ground below the meadow — small tremors, shifts, the deep rumble of something underground deciding what to do next.

The tremor meadow was not a place you walked into without Dallas knowing it was safe.

Vera could see Dallas from the path. She checked her off the list — *Dallas, tremor meadow, watching, good* — and moved on.

Sage noticed that Dallas had been there since before the sun was up.

Pip noticed a squirrel doing something interesting in the branches above and got distracted.

Sage did not laugh. She was watching Dallas.

---

### Chapter 2: The Mother at the Door

The mother deer arrived at the trio's door just as the light grew full.

She had been running. Her sides moved fast and her eyes were wide and she spoke before she had fully stopped.

"My fawn," she said. "She wandered in before I woke. She was curious — I had told her not to go near the meadow, I had told her — the tremor meadow, before dawn—"

"How long?" said Vera.

"Since first light. Maybe before."

Vera was already checking her notes on the tremor meadow's recent pattern. Dallas was the only creature whose antlers could detect a tremor early enough to keep a rescue party safe. Without Dallas, nobody went in.

Vera was already moving toward Dallas.

---

### Chapter 3: The Slow Reading

Dallas heard everything Vera said.

Vera showed her the data — the timing of the last three tremors, the estimated safe windows, the likely shelter spots where a small fawn would stay if frightened. She had it organized and clear.

"We go now," said Vera. "You read the meadow. We follow."

Dallas nodded. She stepped to the edge and raised her antlers.

She waited. And waited. The reading was slow — slow the way Sage was slow, but wrong. It felt muddy. Uncertain.

Her antlers gave a faint signal. She started to take a step in.

The signal shifted. She stopped.

She stepped back.

"I'm not certain," she said quietly. "I'm not — the reading is unclear."

Vera's ears went back. "Try again," she said.

Dallas did not move.

---

### Chapter 4: Pip in the Meadow

Pip looked at the meadow.

He looked at how far in a small fawn could be seen, from here, if you were just very fast.

"I'll go," he said. "I'll be quick. In and out."

Vera put a hand on his arm. "No."

"I'll be careful."

"Pip. Fast and careful in tremor country without a sensor is exactly the thing that causes two emergencies instead of one."

Pip opened his mouth. He closed it. He stood very still for one beat.

"Two emergencies," he said.

"Two emergencies," said Vera.

He looked at the meadow. He looked at Dallas. He did not go into the meadow.

It was one of the harder moments he had had in Whisperwood.

---

### Chapter 5: When Did You Last Sleep

Sage was watching Dallas.

She watched the way Dallas's ears drooped. She watched the way her antlers lowered between readings instead of staying raised. She had seen this before — the same weight Hazel carried after a very long baking night, the same stillness Bram had had behind the tree.

Sage walked up beside Dallas. She did not ask about the tremors. She did not ask about the reading.

"When did you last sleep?" she said.

Dallas blinked.

"I've been on watch since — yesterday morning," she said. "The meadow has been unsettled. I didn't want to leave it without a sensor."

"A whole night," said Sage.

"And the morning before."

Sage looked at her for a long moment. Then she said, gently: "You need to rest."

---

### Chapter 6: While Dallas Slept

Dallas sat down.

Vera put her plan away and looked at the meadow.

Oswin was above them almost immediately — Pip had rung the bellflower before anyone asked him to, and Oswin and two apprentices were already circling the tremor meadow from the air, calling in their careful voices toward the spot where the fawn was visible: a small figure on a raised shelf of ground, sitting very still, frightened but safe.

The bunnies arrived. Bonnie secured the perimeter and marked the evacuation path — three routes out, safe ground to the north.

The trio stayed with Dallas. She slept for two full hours. Real sleep, not one-eye sleep. When she woke, her antlers came up sharp and immediate.

She read the meadow in under a minute.

"Now," she said. "There is a gap. Eleven minutes."

---

### Chapter 7: The Walk Home

Dallas led them in.

She walked slowly, antlers high, reading every tremor through the soles of her hooves and the tips of her antlers. When she stopped, they stopped. When she moved, they followed.

The fawn was on the raised shelf where the owls had kept watch. She was frightened and very patient. When she saw her mother at the meadow's edge, she made a small, high sound.

Dallas guided everyone home between tremors — step by careful step, no rushing. They cleared the meadow just before the next tremor moved through the ground below.

On the path back, a mouse family kept quiet pace at the edge of the trees — Pa steady, Ma's hand on Middle Son's shoulder so he didn't wander toward the interesting meadow, Baby asleep, Big Daughter writing it all down.

"I should have asked how you felt," Vera said to Dallas. "Not whether you were ready."

Dallas looked at her steadily. "You would have known," she said, "if you'd looked."

Dallas turned back to watch the fawn walk ahead of them on the path.

The lantern mushrooms lit the way home.

---
*End of Book 6: The Frightened Fawn*

## About Whisperwood

Whisperwood is an enchanted forest where the enchantment is part of everyday life. Three friends — Vera the fox, Pip the squirrel, and Sage the turtle — solve problems the only way anything ever gets solved here: together, with the right creature, in the right order, one careful step at a time.

## The World of Whisperwood

**Dallas (Head Deer)**
The tremor sensor of Whisperwood. Her antlers detect underground movement before anyone else can feel it. She stands watch at the tremor meadow every day. She cannot sense what she cannot feel — and she cannot feel clearly if she hasn't rested.
*Rule: A sensor has to be rested to read accurately.*

**Owls**
The librarians and scientists of Whisperwood. From the air, they can see what no one on the ground can. Oswin wears spectacles of two curved pieces of bark.
*Rule: Ask them early. They work best with time.*

**Bunnies**
The evacuation specialists. Bonnie knows every safe path — above ground and below. In the tremor meadow, she secures the perimeter and marks the routes out.
*Rule: They plan before the crisis. Don't wait until the tremor starts.*
