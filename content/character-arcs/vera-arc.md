- **Rush:** Vera immediately declares "Flooding. We need the beavers." before asking why the river is rising. She leaves before Sage can speak.

## Arc through Book 2

**Book 2: The Noisy Night**

- **Rush:** Vera is already working the problem before Pip arrives. She has a list and a plan: schedule the birds into a rotation with enforced night-silence hours. She goes to Oswin first — a real change from Book 1. The data-gathering attempt is genuine: she visits the Mapleaf, builds a list (relay pattern, volume, coverage, affected parties), and consults the one creature who tracks the songbird network. She is doing Book 1's lesson. She just applies it incompletely.
- **Wall:** Oswin explains that the noise is a self-reinforcing feedback loop. More order makes it worse — previous interventions by the deer and raccoons amplified the problem. Vera's scheduling plan would do the same. Her pattern recognition fired on the symptom (disordered relay) and missed the root cause (loop dynamics). Physical tells land correctly: ears go back at "What?" then she goes very still. The list fold is canon-consistent (her prop, her stillness enacted physically) and adds visual texture without contradicting anything in the bible.
- **Listen/adjust:** She does not argue with Oswin. She folds the list. In Chapter 7 she defers to Sage's three locations without demanding explanation — "You're certain?" is a check, not a challenge, and when Sage says yes, Vera accepts it and moves to implementation.
- **Acknowledgement:** "I should have asked you first. Before the schedule plan." — directed at Sage specifically, not a general self-correction. Sage's response ("You didn't know what I could hear" / "I didn't ask") sharpens the distinction: Vera knew Sage existed, knew she was present, and still bypassed her. The beat is earned.
- **Arc progression:** Book 1's lesson was *ask why first.* Vera applied it — she asked why, collected data, consulted Oswin. Book 2's lesson is *ask the right who.* She asked a systems expert when the answer required a perceptual witness. The progression is fractional and real: she moved one degree in the right direction and one degree in a new wrong direction. This is exactly the shape the series arc calls for in Books 1–2.
- **Voice:** "I know." / "Probably others too." / "It's a complicated problem." / "They will if I explain it clearly." / "You're certain?" — clipped, declarative, slightly arch. Fully consistent with the bible.
- **Physical tells:** Bag already on shoulder (Chapter 3, before walking toward canopy). Already facing the direction she'll run (turns and walks immediately). Ears back when wrong (Chapter 4). Goes very still (Chapter 4). All canonical.
- **Prop:** List used twice — adding to it in Chapter 3, folding it in Chapter 4. Both uses are load-bearing.
- **Arc status:** Consistent with bible. Fractional forward movement established cleanly. No issues with Vera's characterization.
