## Arc through Book 1

**Book 1: The Rising River**

- **Hangs back:** Sage is first to notice the problem (she watches the water before anyone else), but she observes rather than acts — she rings the bellflower, which is the right tool.
- **Overlooked:** After Vera arrives and immediately declares a plan, Sage opens her mouth and then closes it. She is talked over.
- **Crucial detail:** She takes Pip to the Talking Rock. She presses her hand against it and waits, patiently, for it to speak — one word at a time. This patience reveals the true cause of the flood (the fallen tree).
- **Overwhelm:** Shown briefly in the singing mud scene — "There were a lot of creatures. A lot of sounds. A lot of movement. She took a slow breath." Handled with dignity, not comedy. She knows her part and holds it.
- **Vindication:** Quiet. She says only "Bram will fix the tree tomorrow." Her observation was the turning point, but she does not claim it.
- **Arc status:** Consistent with bible. Her gift (deep listening, patience) is the key to the book's solution. Her overwhelm is present but managed. Pattern established cleanly.
