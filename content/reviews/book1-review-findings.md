## Changes routed to BEATRICE (book edits)

### REQUIRED — hard violations or missing critical content

**R1 — Ch. 6: Sage internal monologue (hard rule violation)**
`"It's okay, she thought. I know what my part is."` is italicised internal monologue — direct violation of the no-internal-monologue rule. Replace with physical beat: Sage pressing one hand against the mud or ground.
*Flagged by: Donna, Sage arc, Florence, Clara, Harriett*

**R2 — Ch. 5: Vera unattributed interior reasoning (rule violation)**
`"Beavers can't move the tree. Not tonight. But the water needs somewhere to go."` and `"A redirect, she thought. Not a fix. Just enough."` — Vera's reasoning told rather than shown. Re-anchor to what she sees on the Mapleaf.
*Flagged by: Donna, Clara, Florence, Harriett*

**R3 — Ch. 1: Sage internal monologue**
`"Too fast, she thought. Something upstream."` — Replace with action: she pulls her foot from the water (which actually already follows, making the thought redundant).
*Flagged by: Harriett, Florence (watch item)*

**R4 — Ch. 7: Root-damage seed missing from prose**
The Book 8 payoff seed (Sage notices old root damage, Bram files it away) exists only in the production note — not in any chapter. Add: Sage noticing the roots + Bram's "Noted." This is a mandatory series continuity beat.
*Flagged by: Sage arc, Donna, Clara, Harriett*

**R5 — Back matter entirely absent (required before publication)**
All three required back matter sections are missing:
- "About Whisperwood" — 2–3 sentences, no spoilers
- "Coming Next" — one-line tease for Book 2: The Noisy Night + cover placeholder
- "The World of Whisperwood" — visual glossary for Book 1's featured resources (Mapleaf, singing mud, Talking Rock, bellflower)
*Flagged by: Harriett*

---

### RECOMMENDED — character canon and series consistency

**C1 — Ch. 2: Vera's list prop never used as action**
The list is mentioned in narration ("making a list of everything she knew") but Vera never physically interacts with it. Add one beat: she crosses something off, or adds a line, before running. Anchors the prop for illustrators and future books.
*Flagged by: Vera arc*

**C2 — Ch. 2: Vera's bag never mentioned**
Vera's bag is a canonical physical signature. One reference in prose (or at minimum an illustration brief) establishes it for the series.
*Flagged by: Vera arc (low priority — could be illustration-only)*

**C3 — Ch. 3: Pip doesn't drop the stick when redirected**
When Sage says "Come with me," Pip just follows — his canonical physical tell (drops whatever he's holding) is absent at its first natural firing. Add: "Pip looked at the stick. He set it down."
*Flagged by: Pip arc*

**C4 — Ch. 3: Pip's stick scene runs parallel to Vera's wall**
Pip's impulsive helping should land after Vera's plan fails, not simultaneously. Add one transitional sentence: "While Vera was gone, Pip was doing what Pip always did when there was a problem."
*Flagged by: Pip arc*

**C5 — Ch. 6: Otter introduced as a list item**
The Otter arrives in one narrative clause, never seen or heard. He's central to Book 4 — his reluctant-but-shows-up character needs to be experienced, not reported. Add one brief beat: a crossed arm, a complaint, something.
*Flagged by: Donna, Clara, Harriett*

**C6 — Ch. 6: Singing mud single-voice ambiguity**
The sequence could read as the mud responding to Vera's solo hum. Add one sentence: "The mud did not move." between her hum and the group joining, to make the one-voice rule legible to young readers.
*Flagged by: Clara*

**C7 — Ch. 7: Pip's "You say that every time" — confusing for ages 4–6**
Past-habit phrasing implies it's happened before, which confuses young readers since this is Book 1. Change to future-tense: "I have a feeling you'll be saying that a lot."
*Flagged by: Vera arc*

**C8 — Ch. 4: Sage's "No. You didn't ask." slightly ahead of her Book 1 register**
Slightly too assertive for early-arc Sage. Soften so contrast with her later books is visible — e.g., she looks at the water instead of at Vera, letting silence do the work.
*Flagged by: Sage arc*

## Change routed to EMILY (bible edit — needs Reena's decision)

**E1 — 8-book vs. 12-book series-scope contradiction**
The bible cover, Section 1, and Section 7 all say "12-book series." Section 5 header says "The 8-Book Arc" and only 8 books are outlined. This causes confusion in every future book and arc review.

**Reena's decision needed:** Is this an 8-book series (fix the cover/intro copy) or a 12-book series (add stubs for Books 9–12 and fix the Section 5 header)?
*Flagged by: Clara*

---

## Florence's watch items for future books (no action needed now)

- **Free indirect discourse convention:** Vera's unitalicised interior reasoning in Ch. 5 is borderline. Establish the convention deliberately in Book 2 rather than inheriting it.
- **Raccoons as community participants:** Fine in Book 1, but protect their specialist identity before Books 3 and 7 make it plot-critical.
- **Glow mushroom vs. glow moss:** Two distinct things in the bible. Future books should not conflate them.
- **Singing mud with partial group:** Consider codifying in the bible what happens if singing stops mid-process or only a partial group sings.
