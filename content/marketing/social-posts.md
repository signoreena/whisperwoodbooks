### Instagram Post 3: Character Spotlight — Vera the Fox

**Caption:**
Meet Vera.

She is a fox. She is approximately 8 years old. She keeps a very long list.

Vera's gift: she sees the structure of a problem before anyone else. She knows which creature to ask, which resource to reach for, and what order things need to go in.

Vera's flaw: she decides the answer before she has heard the whole problem.

She is always sure she is right. She is almost always right about the wrong thing.

Every book in the Whisperwood series ends with a small, quiet version of Vera saying: "I should have asked why first."

She is still learning. So is everyone.

### Instagram Post 11: World-Building — The Singing Mud

**Caption:**
In Whisperwood, the mud at the river's edge responds to voices.

Not to one voice. To many — all together, low and slow, sustained.

When the community hums in a low, coordinated tone, the mud shifts, channels, redirects. When they stop, it settles slowly back to its resting state. No counter-action required.

One voice does nothing.

This is not a magic trick. It is a rule. A feature of the world. And in Book 1, when the river is rising and the beavers cannot help until morning, it is the only tool available.

So Pip goes and finds everyone who can sing.

He is very good at this part.

### Instagram Post 14: Behind the Scenes — The Series Rule We Never Break

**Caption:**
The one rule we never break in Whisperwood:

No single creature solves the whole problem.

Not ever.

Not because we are trying to teach teamwork — though the books do. Not because the characters are not capable — they are extraordinarily capable. Because this is simply the law of this world. The singing mud requires many voices. The beavers need daylight. The talking rocks speak one word at a time. Every specialist has a role and a limit.

Writing within constraints like this is what makes a world feel real. Children who read all eight books will understand Whisperwood the way they understand their own house — the rules, the rhythms, what to reach for and when.

That is the goal.
