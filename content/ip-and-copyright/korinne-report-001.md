## Section 4: Series-Level IP Advisory

### What is protectable across the Whisperwood series

**Prose expression in each book.** Every sentence of the published text is automatically protected from the moment of creation. The git repository — with commit hashes and timestamps — constitutes strong first-fixation evidence. Registration strengthens this by adding public record and statutory damages eligibility.

**The Series Bible as written.** The full Bible document — its character profiles, world rule descriptions, 8-book arc outlines, Mouse Family thread narrative, canon quick-reference, and KDP production notes — is original creative expression fixed in a tangible medium. It is protected from the moment it was saved and committed. A sophisticated infringer who copied the Bible's character designs, world systems, or book arc structure in substantial detail would be copying protected expression, not just unprotectable ideas.

**Illustration art, once created.** Any illustration image — whether created by a human artist, or generated by AI using prompts authored by Reena — is protected visual art from the moment it is fixed (saved as an image file). However:

- If a human illustrator creates the art and is not a work-made-for-hire employee, the illustrator owns the copyright in the art unless there is a written contract assigning those rights to Reena. **Verify that any illustrator contract includes an explicit written assignment of all copyright in all illustrations to Reena Gellerup.** This is a legal document — have an attorney draft or review it.
- For AI-generated images: as of the documentation date (2026-05-23), US copyright law and USCO guidance require meaningful human authorship for registration. The extent to which AI-generated images qualify depends on the degree of human creative input (selection, arrangement, editing, iteration). The illustration prompts themselves are Reena's original expression and are protected. Consult an attorney on the current state of AI copyright guidance before attempting to register the illustrations.

**The Mouse Family narrative thread.** The Mouse Family visual storyline — five specific characters with specific visual signatures, specific emotional beats across 8 books, and the closing image of Baby Mouse awake for the first time — is original creative expression documented in the Series Bible. It is protected as written.

**Character expression as written.** Vera, Pip, and Sage as specifically described and depicted in the books are protected. The physical tells, voice patterns, and per-book character arcs documented in the Bible are protected expression.

The following are ideas, concepts, or facts — not protectable by copyright regardless of registration:

### Trademark advisory: "WHISPERWOOD" as a series mark

**Recommendation: File a trademark application. The timing depends on publication status.**

"WHISPERWOOD" as a series identifier used in commerce on books and merchandise is a strong trademark candidate. It is:
- Arbitrary or fanciful (not a descriptive word in the children's book market)
- Not a common word in the relevant class
- Likely to be distinctive in commerce

**Search first.** Before filing, search the USPTO TESS database at tmsearch.uspto.gov for "WHISPERWOOD" in Class 16 (printed books, paper goods) and Class 28 (toys, games — relevant if merchandise is planned). A preliminary search shows no obvious conflicts based on the name, but this is not a substitute for a proper clearance search. An IP attorney or trademark agent should conduct a full clearance search before filing.

**Filing timing:**
- If books are not yet published and not yet in commerce: file a **1B (Intent to Use)** application now. This reserves your priority date and gives you 6 months (extendable) to begin use before the mark must be in actual commerce.
- If books are published and the series name is in use in commerce (on KDP, on the website): file a **1A (Use in Commerce)** application immediately.
- Class 16 (printed books) is the essential class. Class 28 (merchandise, toys) is worth considering if plush toys, games, or other merchandise are planned even in the medium term.
- TEAS Plus fee: $250 per class. Two classes = $500.

**Attorney recommendation:** Trademark filing is more consequential than copyright registration — an error in the application, a conflict you missed, or a poorly drafted goods/services description can lose you the mark or limit its scope. An IP attorney is strongly recommended for the filing itself. The attorney fee for a straightforward trademark application typically runs $500–$1,500 on top of USPTO fees.

**The trademark notice.** The copyright page of each book already includes "Whisperwood is a trademark of Reena Gellerup." This is appropriate and costs nothing. Use "TM" (unregistered) now; switch to the registered mark symbol (R in a circle) only after USPTO registration is granted.

### Comp title use on the website and KDP listings

**Confirmed: the following comp title references are legally permissible.**

The Series Bible and marketing materials reference these comp titles: "The Princess in Black" (Hale/Ostertag), "Frog and Toad" (Lobel), "Owl at Home" (Lobel). Using these in marketing is standard publishing practice and is protected as comparative reference under US law. Titles are not copyrightable.

**How to phrase them correctly:**

Safe and recommended:
- "Perfect for readers who love The Princess in Black"
- "In the tradition of Arnold Lobel's Frog and Toad"
- "Fans of Frog and Toad and The Princess in Black will find a new home in Whisperwood"
- "Comp titles: The Princess in Black (Hale/Ostertag), Frog and Toad (Lobel)" — this exact phrasing is fine on a KDP description or media kit

What to avoid:
- Do not reproduce cover art, interior illustrations, or substantial text from any comp title on the website or in marketing
- Do not write or imply any endorsement: "as recommended by Shannon Hale" or "Arnold Lobel would love this" — unless you have that in writing
- Do not use the publisher's logo or a series logo of a comp title in a way that suggests affiliation or endorsement
- Do not use the phrase "the next Frog and Toad" in a way that implies official successor status (acceptable as informal hyperbole in conversation; avoid it in formal copy)

The website at whisperwoodbooks.com may freely name comp titles in the "About the Series" or marketing copy sections. KDP product descriptions may include comp title references. These are standard, legal, and beneficial for discoverability.
