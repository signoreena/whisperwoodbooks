## Proposal 2 — 2026-05-23

**Proposed by:** Reena (series author — direct instruction)
**Regarding:** Section 3 callout (Narrative Voice Rule) and Section 6 Rules That Cannot Be Broken — internal monologue rule
**Summary of change:** Soften the absolute ban on italicised internal thought to permit it sparingly as texture when paired with physical action, while preserving the core principle that action is always the primary vehicle for character motivation.
**Reason given:** Reena has clarified that the rule was too absolute. An occasional italicised thought paired with physical action is acceptable — the problem is internal thought used as the sole or primary way to convey a character's inner state.

**Emily's decision:** APPROVED — directed by Reena

**Reasoning:** The refined rule is more precise and more useful than the original blanket prohibition. It preserves the series' core commercial and craft commitment — action-driven storytelling, no shortcuts, young-reader accessibility — while giving writers a realistic and workable standard. The new formulation ("never as the sole or primary signal") is a stronger and more enforceable rule than "no italicised thought" because it explains the actual problem rather than prohibiting a technique categorically. This makes the bible a better working document without weakening the series' identity. Appropriate for ages 4–6, consistent with comp titles, no sellability risk.

**What was changed (Emily made these edits directly per Reena's instruction):**

Place A — Section 3, callout box:
- Label changed from `Narrative Voice Rule — No Internal Monologue` to `Narrative Voice Rule — Action Over Internal Monologue`
- Body paragraph replaced with the softened rule specifying that italicised thought is acceptable sparingly when paired with physical action, but may never be the sole or primary signal of inner state.

Place B — Section 6, Rules That Cannot Be Broken list:
- Item changed from `Character motivation is shown through action, never explained through internal monologue. No italicised thought.` to `Character motivation is shown through action. Italicised internal thought may be used sparingly as texture — never as the sole or primary signal of what a character perceives or feels.`
