## The Builder's Big Blunder — Cover: The Structure Falls

**Page location:** Cover
**Scene:** The storage structure is in the moment of collapse — the central beam cracking, one wall leaning, the roof beginning to drop. The meadow birds stand in a circle watching. Bram stands at the edge of the scene, very still. The cover conveys the moment just after something has gone wrong.
**Prompt:** Children's picture book cover illustration, 5.06 by 7.81 inches portrait, no bleed. Sharp editorial linework with warmth — Quentin Blake meets Edward Ardizzone. Open meadow, morning light. The wooden storage structure — a well-made building of interlocked timber, curved roof — is mid-collapse: the central beam cracks with a sharp visual line, one side wall leans outward, the roof drops. Small bundles of seeds and dried berries scatter from the collapsing chambers. Around it in a loose circle: meadow birds, small and still, watching. At the right edge of the image, standing completely still: Bram the beaver — stout, bark hard hat, hands at his sides, expression unreadable but weighted. He is not surprised. He already knew.
