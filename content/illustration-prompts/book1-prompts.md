# Book 1: The Rising River — Illustration Prompts

*9 chapters · 10 illustration spreads · Written for AI image generation*
*Style: Sharp editorial linework, rich forest greens and gold, warm painterly color, clean edges. All characters must read as their species at thumbnail size.*

## Spread 1 — Opening Establishing Spread
**Chapter 1 · Full-bleed double-page spread**

**Scene:** An ordinary Tuesday morning at the river's edge in Whisperwood — all three characters in their natural registers before anything has gone wrong.

**Characters present:**
- Vera (foreground right): Sitting with her back against the enormous trunk of the Mapleaf tree, one leg tucked, list in her paw. She is mid-addition, pen moving. Her satchel rests against her hip. Eyes scanning upward at the Mapleaf's veins — routine, confident, calm. Already slightly arch in expression.
- Pip (mid-ground upper left): Mid-leap between two branches, arms out, eyes wide, tail arcing behind him. One hind foot still grazing a branch; the other leg already committed to the air. A large fallen branch on the ground below him hints he has already been down once. Expression: pure concentrated joy.
- Sage (foreground left, lower than the others): Crouched at the waterline, one hand flat on a river rock, watching tadpoles in the shallows. The stillest thing in the image. Her dark eyes are fixed just below the water surface. Not interacting with the others — she is entirely absorbed.

**Setting:** The Mapleaf tree on the left-center, its enormous leaves spreading across the top third of the spread. The river runs across the lower edge, calm and clear. Dense forest canopy in the background. The hedgehog baker's stall is faintly visible in the far background — small rolls stacked on a flat stone counter. Two sparrows are mid-argument in the upper canopy, one pointing a wing at the other.

**World elements shown:**
- The Mapleaf tree: enormous, leaves spanning wide, leaf-veins glowing soft gold-green — healthy and calm. This is important: the veins must read as luminous but normal here. They will go dark in Spread 2.
- The river: clear, gentle current, reflective. Tadpoles visible as small dark commas just below the surface near Sage.
- Lantern mushrooms: small clusters growing at the base of the Mapleaf trunk and along the near riverbank — warm amber glow, even in morning light.

**Mouse family placement:** Background, center-left. The mouse family is walking the high path above the river bank, going about morning errands. Ma Mouse carries a small framed painting under one arm — a tiny portrait of a cozy burrow. Pa Mouse walks beside her with practiced calm. Big Daughter has a tiny notebook open, writing as she walks. Middle Son has turned his whole body to watch Pip leap overhead. Baby is asleep on Pa's back in a bundle.

**Mood/lighting:** Clear morning — early sun coming through the canopy in warm shafts, dappled gold-green on everything. Peaceful, domestic, completely ordinary. The warmth of a morning that has no idea what's about to happen.

**Action:** Vera writes; Pip leaps; Sage watches. No character is aware of the others' activity.

**Style note:** Sharp editorial linework, rich forest colors, clean edges, anthropomorphic animals legible at thumbnail size.

**Full prompt:**
A warm morning in Whisperwood enchanted forest. Full-bleed double-page spread. A young female fox in a satchel (Vera) sits against the trunk of an enormous tree with golden-veined luminous leaves, writing on a long list. Upper left, a young male grey squirrel (Pip) is mid-leap between branches, arms wide, bushy tail arcing, mouth open in concentration. Lower left, a young female turtle (Sage) crouches at a clear river's edge, one hand flat on a mossy stone, watching tadpoles — the stillest figure in the scene; long eyelashes, her only feminine marker. Morning sunlight filters through a dense forest canopy. Clusters of small amber-glowing lantern mushrooms dot the base of the great tree. In the far background, a mouse family walks a high path — the mother carrying a tiny framed painting, the father calm, a daughter writing in a notebook, a young son craning to watch the squirrel, a baby asleep on the father's back. Sharp editorial linework, rich forest greens and gold, painterly, clean edges, characters clearly readable as their animal species.

## Spread 2 — The Mapleaf Warns
**Chapter 1 · Right-page spread**

**Scene:** Vera looks up from her list to find the Mapleaf's veins have gone dark — the signal that something is coming.

**Characters present:**
- Vera (center): Standing, list still in one paw, already half-turned toward the river. Her free paw is raised slightly — she has just pushed off the tree. Her eyes are up, fixed on the veins. Her expression has shifted: sharp, alert, calculating. She is not frightened. She is already thinking.
- Pip (upper right, just entering the frame): Falling from a branch toward Vera — arms out, eyes wide. He is about to land on her head. His mouth is open mid-word: "Something is coming."

**Setting:** The Mapleaf tree filling the left side and top — now with veins darkened to deep green-black, the glow gone cold. The change should be visually dramatic: the left panel of this spread has veins dark, while the memory of their gold warmth is still in the background lantern mushroom glow.

**World elements shown:**
- The Mapleaf tree: veins now dark green-black, visibly changed from golden. The shift is the whole point — the illustrator should show the contrast between dark veins and the warm glow of the forest around them.
- Lantern mushrooms at the base of the tree still glow, unchanged — they are not reading the Mapleaf, only the tree's veins have reacted.

**Mouse family placement:** Not in this spread.

**Mood/lighting:** The quality of light has not changed, but the Mapleaf's shift creates a visual shadow effect — as if a small, localized cloud has passed over the tree's inner light. The rest of the forest is still warm and morning-bright.

**Action:** Vera mid-pivot, already turning; Pip in freefall.

**Illustrator flag:** The before/after of the Mapleaf veins (gold in Spread 1, dark here) is one of the book's key visual payoffs. Make the dark veins read as clearly wrong — they should feel like a change in a face the reader has come to know.

**Style note:** Sharp editorial linework, rich forest colors, clean edges, anthropomorphic animals legible at thumbnail size.

**Full prompt:**
A young female fox (Vera) stands at the base of an enormous magical tree, looking up at its leaves with sharp alert eyes — her list still in one paw, already turning away from the tree. The tree's leaf-veins, which were gold-green in the morning, have turned dark deep green-black, their glow extinguished — a visual warning. From above right, a young grey squirrel (Pip) is falling toward her, arms spread wide, mouth open mid-sentence. Morning forest light around them is still warm and gold, making the dark-veined tree more striking by contrast. Small clusters of amber lantern mushrooms glow at the tree's roots. Sharp editorial linework, rich greens and gold, clean edges, characters legible as their animal species at thumbnail size.

## Spread 3 — Sage at the Water with the Bellflower
**Chapter 2 · Full-bleed double-page spread**

**Scene:** Sage has noticed the river is rising — wrong, slow, inexorable — and is ringing a bellflower to call the others.

**Characters present:**
- Sage (left-center, foreground): Standing at the very edge of the water. One foot is just withdrawn from the current — still damp. Her posture is alert but not panicked; she has moved from watching to deciding. In her right hand she holds a bellflower stem, extended out over the water, mid-ring — the bell at the end is caught mid-sway. Her dark eyes are steady, watching the water level. She is the calmest creature in a scene that is not calm.

**Setting:** River's edge, mid-morning. The water is visibly higher than it should be — the waterline is a few inches above the usual mark on a mossy stone nearby (illustrator: this is a subtle detail, not dramatic flooding yet — the wrong-ness is in how still and high and purposeful the water looks). The far bank is reflected in the water. Bellflowers grow in small clusters along the bank on long trembling stems.

**World elements shown:**
- Bellflowers: delicate bell-shaped flowers on long stems, several visible along the bank. The one Sage holds is extended over the water — its bell mid-swing, a tiny implied ring-line visible around it.
- The river: higher than normal. No churning, no white water — the wrongness is its quiet, inevitable rise. The waterline is visibly covering the roots of a riverside shrub that should be above water.

**Mouse family placement:** Far background, right side. The mouse family is visible on the high path above the opposite bank — tiny, distant. Middle Son is pointing at the water. Ma Mouse has a firm hand on his shoulder without looking at him; her eyes are ahead. The framed painting is under her other arm.

**Mood/lighting:** Mid-morning light, slightly cooler than Spread 1 — the forest senses something. The colors are still rich but the gold has faded slightly. The water has a grey-green tint to it rather than the clear-reflection warmth of Spread 1.

**Action:** Sage holds the bellflower mid-ring, one foot just drawn back from the current, eyes on the water.

**Style note:** Sharp editorial linework, rich forest colors, clean edges, anthropomorphic animals legible at thumbnail size.

**Full prompt:**
A young female turtle (Sage) stands alone at the edge of a river in a magical forest, holding a delicate bellflower on a long stem extended over the water — the bell caught mid-swing, just rung. One foot is still damp from the current she just stepped back from. Her dark eyes are calm and steady, fixed on the water level; long eyelashes are her only feminine feature. The river is visibly higher than it should be — still and purposeful, not churning, the roots of a riverside shrub submerged in water that should be air. Bellflowers grow in clusters along the bank. In the far background across the water, tiny figures of a mouse family on a high path — the mother carrying a small framed painting, a young son pointing at the water and being redirected by her hand. Mid-morning forest light, slightly cooler than morning gold. Sharp editorial linework, rich forest greens, clean edges, characters legible as their species.

## Spread 4 — Pip with the Stick; Sage Goes Still
**Chapter 3 / Chapter 4 · Right-page spread**

**Scene:** Pip has his large stick wedged in the riverbank and is trying to help with it; Sage is watching him with patient stillness, about to redirect him.

**Characters present:**
- Pip (center-left): Leaning hard into the large stick — both hands wrapped around it, shoulder-width stance, leaning his whole body against it. His face is set with determination. The stick is wedged in the soft mud of the bank at an angle, going nowhere. His tail is arced over his head with effort. He does not look like he knows what he expects to happen, but he is committed.
- Sage (right): Completely still. One hand resting flat on a nearby tree root. Watching Pip. Her expression is not critical — just observing. She has already decided something. Her next step is visible in how her body weight has shifted slightly toward him.

**Setting:** The river's edge — the water has risen noticeably from Spread 3. The muddy bank is soft and churned in places. Vera's footprints in the mud point away from the scene — she has already left.

**World elements shown:**
- The river: visibly higher — water lapping closer to the path than normal.
- The singing mud: the bank mud is dark and rich-looking, slightly shifting at the water's edge in a way that suggests potential — it is not responding to Pip's stick, but it has a quality of readiness to it.

**Mouse family placement:** Far background, lower right — the mouse family is on the move with bundles. They are clearly evacuating, not watching: Ma has the framed painting pressed to her chest with both hands, Pa has the baby-bundle on his back and two tied cloth bags in his arms, Big Daughter has her notebook out and is writing while walking, Middle Son is craning over his shoulder toward Pip and the stick. They are heading toward higher ground.

**Mood/lighting:** Afternoon light — the river crisis is in progress. Light is still warm but there is urgency in the shadows. The mud is darker, the water higher.

**Action:** Pip leaning with full effort into a useless stick; Sage perfectly still beside him, having made a quiet decision.

**Illustrator flag:** The contrast between Pip's full-body effort and Sage's complete stillness is the emotional core of this spread. They should both read clearly — the comedy of the stick and the wisdom of the stillness.

**Style note:** Sharp editorial linework, rich forest colors, clean edges, anthropomorphic animals legible at thumbnail size.

**Full prompt:**
At a river's edge in an enchanted forest, a young grey squirrel (Pip) leans his whole body into a large stick wedged in the muddy bank at an angle — both hands gripping it, tail arced overhead with effort, face set in pure determination, the stick going nowhere. Beside him stands a young turtle (Sage), completely still, one hand resting flat on a tree root, watching him with calm patient eyes and long eyelashes — the most motionless creature in the image. The river behind them has risen noticeably, water lapping close to the path. The mud of the bank is dark and rich, slightly reflective. In the far background, a mouse family moves with bundles toward higher ground — the mother holding a small framed painting to her chest, the father with a baby asleep on his back, a daughter writing in a notebook while walking, a young son looking back at the squirrel. Late afternoon forest light, warm but urgent. Sharp editorial linework, rich forest greens and gold, clean edges, characters legible as their species at thumbnail size.

## Spread 5 — KEY IMAGE: Sage Hand on Boulder, Pip Nose-to-Rock
**Chapter 4 · Full-bleed double-page spread**

**Scene:** Sage has her hand flat on the oldest boulder at the river's edge, eyes closed, asking it a question; Pip has leaned in until his nose almost touches the rock — waiting, listening.

**Characters present:**
- Sage (left, foreground): Both hands now flat on the great boulder, eyes closed, face angled slightly down toward the rock. Her expression is one of deep focused listening — the most intent she has been in the book. She is in the boulder's world now. Her shell, with its pattern, is visible from the side. She is the stillest thing in the image.
- Pip (right, foreground): Crouched low, nose millimeters from the boulder's surface, eyes very wide. He has leaned in without knowing why — it just seems right. His tail is low and still for once. He is holding his breath. His earlier stick is on the ground behind him, forgotten.

**Setting:** The oldest boulder at the river's edge — large, round, covered in deep green and grey moss. It has been in this spot since before anyone can remember. It radiates age. The river flows behind it. The water level here is higher; the ground around the boulder's base is damp.

**World elements shown:**
- The talking rock: round, massive, entirely moss-covered. Ancient. The moss is soft and layered — it has the quality of something very old and very patient. There is a faint warmth to the stone that the illustrator should suggest through color: the moss closest to the rock's surface has a very slightly warmer tone than the moss at the edges.
- The river: visible behind the boulder, higher than normal. The singing mud of the bank nearby is dark.
- Lantern mushrooms: one small cluster growing at the boulder's base, adding a warm amber point of light.

**Mouse family placement:** Visible in the background, moving past in the middle distance — the mouse family hurrying past below, bundles on their backs, quiet and fast. This is their "hurried past" moment from the prose. They are not looking at the rock — they have their own problem. Baby is asleep. Middle Son is the only one who glances toward the boulder as they pass.

**Mood/lighting:** Dusk approaching — the light is lower and more directional, coming from the right (west). The forest has darkened slightly. The lantern mushrooms at the boulder's base glow more visibly now that the ambient light has softened. The scene has a hush to it — the visual quiet of the moment before a rock speaks.

**Action:** Sage hands flat, eyes closed; Pip nose-close, eyes open wide.

**Illustrator flag:** This is the book's key image — the one that should carry emotional weight on its own, without text. The two characters' relationship with the rock is different: Sage is communicating; Pip is witnessing. The rock is not showing a face or expression — it is just a rock, old and huge and slightly warm. The weight of the moment is entirely in the two children leaning toward it.

**Style note:** Sharp editorial linework, rich forest colors, clean edges, anthropomorphic animals legible at thumbnail size.

**Full prompt:**
The most important image in the book. A young turtle girl (Sage) stands with both hands pressed flat against a massive ancient moss-covered boulder at a river's edge, eyes closed, long eyelashes visible, face angled toward the rock in deep concentrated listening. Beside her, a young grey squirrel boy (Pip) has crouched low and leaned in until his nose nearly touches the moss — eyes enormous and wide, tail stilled for once, holding his breath. The boulder is enormous, round, layered in deep green-grey moss, clearly ancient — it radiates patience and age. A single small cluster of amber lantern mushrooms glows at its base. The river flows behind them, noticeably high. In the middle distance, a mouse family hurries past in the background with bundles on their backs — one young son glancing back at the boulder as they go, baby asleep on father's back. Dusk light from the right, forest deepening, the lantern mushrooms warm against the cooling air. Hush and stillness. Sharp editorial linework, rich forest greens and amber, clean edges, characters fully legible as their species.

## Spread 6 — The Wall: Vera's Ears Go Back
**Chapter 4 · Right-page spread**

**Scene:** Vera stands in front of Bram at the beavers' work area by the river, trying to get his help — and he has just said no. Her ears have gone back.

**Characters present:**
- Vera (foreground left): Standing, bag on shoulder, list still in paw. Her ears are back — flat against her head, just slightly, the involuntary tell. Her mouth is open; she was mid-argument. Her body is still facing him fully — she has not retreated — but the ears are back and her eyes have gone still. This is the first time in the book she does not have an answer.
- Bram (center-right): A large beaver in a hard hat made of bark — the hat is practical-looking, worn, clearly custom-fitted. His expression is the face of a creature who has seen many floods. He is not unkind. He is stroking his chin. He is looking at Vera with an expression that says: I understand this is urgent and I am still saying no.

**Setting:** The beavers' dam and work area at the river — massive interlocked logs and branches, worn smooth, structurally impressive. Organized piles of materials. The beavers are a working operation. The light is fading — this is evening.

**World elements shown:**
- The beavers' dam: visible in the background — a complex, well-built structure of interlocked branches and mud. Impressive. Professional.
- Lantern mushrooms: hung from the dam structure in small clusters, providing working light for the evening shift. They cast the scene in warm amber.

**Mouse family placement:** Not in this spread.

**Mood/lighting:** Evening — the natural light is blue-grey, making the lantern mushroom glow more vivid. The beaver work area has a practical, purposeful quality to it. This is not a cozy scene. This is where real work happens.

**Action:** Vera's ears back, mouth open mid-argument; Bram chin-stroking, firm, kind.

**Illustrator flag:** Vera's ears going back is the most important physical character moment in the book for her. It must read clearly but subtly — not dramatically flattened, just moved back from their usual forward-alert position. The reader should be able to see she is not defeated, just caught. Her body is still there, facing the problem.

**Style note:** Sharp editorial linework, rich forest colors, clean edges, anthropomorphic animals legible at thumbnail size.

**Full prompt:**
Evening at a beaver's work dam in an enchanted forest. A young female fox (Vera) stands with her satchel on her shoulder and a list in her paw, facing a large beaver (Bram) who wears a practical bark hard hat. Her fox ears have moved back — not flat, just slightly pinned — the involuntary tell of being caught wrong. Her mouth is open mid-argument; her eyes have gone still and calculating. Bram strokes his chin with one broad paw, expression patient and unmovable — a creature who has seen many floods and knows what cannot be rushed. Behind them, the beaver dam rises — interlocked branches and log, well-built, professional. Small clusters of amber lantern mushrooms hang from the dam structure, casting warm working light against the blue-grey evening sky. Sharp editorial linework, rich forest colors, clean edges, fox and beaver clearly legible as their species at thumbnail size.

## Spread 7 — Community Singing at the River's Edge
**Chapter 7 · Full-bleed double-page spread**

**Scene:** The community has gathered at the river's edge to sing to the mud — a warm, chaotic, everyone-present moment as the temporary channel opens.

**Characters present:**
- Vera (front-center): Standing at the water's edge, facing the group. She has just hummed the first note — her mouth is still slightly open from it, her eyes scanning the assembled creatures. Ears forward, bag on shoulder. She is coordinating, not singing alone.
- Pip (front-left): Mid-song, singing with his whole body — mouth wide open, tail raised, one foot planted, one foot lifted. Slightly to the left of pitch in posture too. He is giving everything.
- Sage (back-right edge of the group): Has taken one step back from the crowd noise. Her face is concentrated — not overwhelmed, choosing her moment. She is looking at the mud, not at the group. Her hand is at her side, not yet on anything — she is about to move forward. This is the moment just before she finds her part.
- Bram (left-center): Beside two other beavers, singing low and steady. His hard bark hat. His face says: I have done stranger things.
- The otter (right-center): Arms crossed. Mouth open — reluctantly singing. Expression is pure "I am doing this under protest." But the otter is there, and singing.
- Two deer (background): Heads raised, antlers catching the light, singing together. Their size makes them visible above the crowd.
- Three raccoons (mid-ground): Singing in practiced unison, slightly to the left of center.
- Several other forest creatures (background): Various sizes and species filling the crowd — bunnies, birds, hedgehog. The scene should feel full and slightly chaotic.

**Setting:** The river's edge at night — the water is high, the bank dark and churned. The singing mud at the water's edge is beginning to shift — a small channel is just starting to open toward the left, dark mud curling up in low walls on either side. The mossy clearing is faintly visible in the upper left background as the destination.

**World elements shown:**
- Singing mud: visibly responding — the mud at the water's edge is in motion, slow and purposeful. Low walls forming on either side of a nascent channel. Dark, rich, alive.
- Lantern mushrooms: carried by some creatures and growing along the path — the whole scene is lit by their warm amber glow. This is a night scene lit primarily by lantern mushrooms.
- The river: high, dark, pressing.

**Mouse family placement:** Left background, clearly visible but not singing — they are watching from a slight distance, just above the action on higher ground. Their bundles are at their feet. Middle Son has his mouth slightly open as if he almost wants to join in. Ma has a firm hand on his shoulder. Big Daughter is writing. Pa is holding Baby — asleep — and watching the mud move with great calm. Ma's framed painting is propped against a tree root beside their pile.

**Mood/lighting:** Night — lit entirely by lantern mushrooms carried by the crowd and growing along the bank. Warm amber against dark blue-green forest shadows. Chaotic but purposeful. This is messy, improvised, community-made magic.

**Action:** Every creature singing; mud just beginning to move; channel starting to form.

**Illustrator flag:** The mud must visibly respond — not dramatically, but visibly. Low walls curving left toward the mossy clearing. This is not a tidy channel. It is the beginning of one: curved, irregular, made by group voices. The chaos of the crowd and the order of the mud should contrast — the mud is doing something beautiful and the crowd is a glorious mess.

**Style note:** Sharp editorial linework, rich forest colors, clean edges, anthropomorphic animals legible at thumbnail size.

**Full prompt:**
Night at a river's edge in an enchanted forest. A large community of forest animals sings together in warm amber lantern-mushroom light. In front: a young female fox (Vera) coordinates with open mouth, ears forward; beside her a young grey squirrel (Pip) sings with his whole body, mouth wide, tail raised, utterly committed. At the right edge, a young turtle (Sage) stands one step back from the crowd, eyes fixed on the mud — long eyelashes, her only feminine marker — choosing her moment. A large beaver in a bark hard hat (Bram) sings steadily beside two other beavers. An otter sings with arms crossed and an expression of pure reluctant participation. Two deer raise their heads and antlers behind the crowd. Three raccoons sing in unison. The dark rich mud at the water's edge is in motion — low curved walls forming, a small channel beginning to open toward the left. Lantern mushrooms on stems and carried by creatures light the whole scene in warm amber. In the background on higher ground, a mouse family watches: the mother's hand on her young son's shoulder, the father holding a sleeping baby, the daughter writing in a notebook. Framed painting propped against a tree root. Night forest, chaotic and warm, community magic. Sharp editorial linework, rich colors, clean edges.

## Spread 8 — First Light at the River Bend
**Chapter 8 · Right-page spread**

**Scene:** Dawn at the river bend — Bram and three beavers work on the fallen oak, and the river begins to run free.

**Characters present:**
- Bram (foreground left): Standing in the water up to his knees, directing — one arm extended, pointing along the log. His bark hard hat. His expression is entirely focused, matter-of-fact. This is his book's great moment and he is treating it like a Tuesday.
- Three beavers (mid-ground): Working on the fallen oak — two at the water, one above on the bank. Tools in use: the flat of strong tails as levers, broad paws braced on the log. The log is enormous. The effort is real and communal.

**Setting:** The river bend — the great fallen oak lies heavy across the channel, its root ball visible on the left bank, roots torn and old (subtly: show the root ball, and show that the roots are weathered and weakened — old damage, not clean breakage. This is a detail that pays off in Book 8). The channel beyond the log is partially cleared. Early dawn light — pale gold-pink just touching the tops of the trees, grey-blue below.

**World elements shown:**
- The fallen oak: enormous, across the channel, partially submerged. Root ball visible, roots showing old damage. The log is being levered up and shifted.
- The river: pressing against the log, beginning to find a way through the cleared channel — the water downstream of the log shows the first quickening of flow. The direction of pressure tells the story.
- Lantern mushrooms: a few still glowing at the bank — they have been here all night; they will fade as full day comes.

**Mouse family placement:** Not in this spread. (Their story is moving forward above ground; they are settling into temporary nests. Their visual beat comes in the following spread.)

**Mood/lighting:** Dawn — the earliest first light. Cold blue-grey ground light with a pink-gold touch at the canopy. The lantern mushrooms are still glowing but slightly dimmed by the arriving day. Quiet, focused, purposeful. The contrast of the huge log and the small beavers working together.

**Action:** Bram directing; three beavers levering the fallen oak; river just beginning to quicken through the clearing channel.

**Illustrator flag:** The root ball of the fallen oak should show old damage — weathered, darkened roots that have been weakening for a long time. This is not a dramatic detail in this book, but it must be accurate because it matters in Book 8. Show it clearly enough that a reader returning to Book 1 after Book 8 would see it.

**Style note:** Sharp editorial linework, rich forest colors, clean edges, anthropomorphic animals legible at thumbnail size.

**Full prompt:**
Dawn at a river bend in a magical forest. A large beaver (Bram) stands knee-deep in the river in a hard hat made of bark, one arm extended, directing three other beavers who work on an enormous fallen oak log that lies heavy across the channel. The beavers use their broad tails and strong paws as levers against the log. The oak's root ball is visible at the left bank — the roots large, old, clearly long-weakened before the break. The river is pressing against the log; in the downstream channel beyond, the water is just beginning to quicken and run as the log shifts. A few amber lantern mushrooms still glow on the bank, dimming in the first light. Early dawn: cold blue-grey light below, a thin warm gold-pink just touching the canopy tops. The work is quiet and focused. The gap is just beginning to open. Sharp editorial linework, rich forest colors and early morning light, clean edges, beavers legible as their species.

## Spread 9 — The Burrows Drain; Mouse Family Above Ground
**Chapter 8 · Full-bleed double-page spread (or right-page)**

**Scene:** Morning — the water has receded, burrows draining, and the mouse family is settled in a temporary above-ground nest with their things around them. The framed painting is up on the tree beside them.

**Characters present:**
- The mouse family (center-foreground): In their temporary above-ground shelter — a wide-roofed space between two tree roots, a piece of bark propped for extra cover. Ma Mouse is hanging the framed painting on a small nail in the bark wall — she has clearly done this first, before unpacking anything else. Pa Mouse sits with Baby on his lap, perfectly calm. Baby is asleep. Big Daughter sits with her notebook open, writing. Middle Son stands at the edge of the shelter, watching the direction of the river — he can see the beavers' work from here. He is not supposed to go look. He is thinking about going to look.

**Setting:** Above-ground temporary shelter in the upper clearing — the mossy clearing where the singing mud redirected the water. The channel of singing mud has already settled back into the bank. The burrow entrances below are visible in the mid-ground, water still draining out of them in small streams. The hedgehog baker's morning rolls are visible in the far background — the ordinary world reasserting itself.

**World elements shown:**
- The singing mud channel: now settled, visibly returning to the bank as a slightly irregular scar in the earth — the mud is settling back, the channel closing. The temporary channel is over.
- The framed painting: small, in a simple frame, depicting a cozy burrow interior — being hung with care on a bark wall. Ma Mouse's most important possession.
- Lantern mushrooms: a few at the base of the great tree roots, still providing some warmth in the morning light.

**Mouse family placement:** They ARE the subject of this spread — center and foreground. This is their moment.

**Mood/lighting:** Full morning — warm, restored, the ordinary light of a day after. The crisis is over. The light is clear and gold again.

**Action:** Ma Mouse hanging the painting; Pa sitting with sleeping Baby; Big Daughter writing; Middle Son watching the horizon longingly.

**Illustrator flag:** The framed painting must be readable as a small painting of a burrow home — a cozy underground interior visible in miniature. This is the family's most important object across all 8 books. It should look like something worth carrying through a flood.

**Style note:** Sharp editorial linework, rich forest colors, clean edges, anthropomorphic animals legible at thumbnail size.

**Full prompt:**
Morning in an enchanted forest clearing. A mouse family has settled in a temporary above-ground shelter formed between two large gnarled tree roots, a flat piece of bark propped for a roof. The mother mouse is carefully hanging a small framed painting — a tiny portrait of a cozy burrow home — on the bark wall, first thing, before anything else is unpacked. The father mouse sits calmly on a root with a sleeping baby bundled on his lap, expression of deep practiced calm. A daughter mouse with a tiny notebook writes in it, seated cross-legged.
