## The Noisy Night — Cover: Sage at the Forked Elm

**Page location:** Cover
**Scene:** Sage stands alone at the base of the great forked elm in the forest at night, one hand pressed to the bark, chin slightly raised, listening through an enormous sound. The canopy above her is alive with overlapping birds in motion. The scene conveys stillness at the center of chaos.
**Prompt:** Children's picture book cover illustration, 5.06 by 7.81 inches portrait, no bleed. Sharp editorial linework with warmth — Quentin Blake meets Edward Ardizzone. Night forest scene. Sage, a small green turtle girl with a patterned shell and calm dark eyes, stands at the base of a great forked elm tree, one palm pressed flat against the bark, chin slightly tilted up, expression of absolute focused stillness. Above her in the canopy, a swirling relay of small songbirds in overlapping flight — each one slightly higher and louder than the last, a visual spiral of escalating noise. The forest is night-dark and blue-green. The birds are warm-colored, energetic, overlapping. Sage is the still point in the center of it all. Warm moonlight through the canopy.
