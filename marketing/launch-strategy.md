# Whisperwood Books — Launch Strategy
*Prepared by Jeanine · May 26, 2026*

## What the Research Found

**Amazon KDP (2026)**
- The A10 algorithm actively rewards external traffic. Books that bring visitors from Instagram, Pinterest, or TikTok to Amazon receive a ranking boost because Amazon is getting new customers it didn't generate itself. Social media efforts directly affect Amazon rank.
- For children's ebooks, file size compression matters enormously for royalties. A 10MB file at $2.99 earns about $0.59/sale after delivery fees. A compressed 3MB file at the same price earns $1.64. Compress epub images to stay under 3MB per book.
- KDP Select (Kindle Unlimited + Free Days) is worth enrolling in for Books 1 and 2 at launch. Borrows contribute to sales rank, and Free Book days in the first week create an immediate velocity spike that helps organic ranking. Tradeoff: 90-day Amazon exclusivity for the ebook.
- Start ebook price at $0.99–$2.99 for the first two weeks to build velocity and rank, then move to $4.99. Paperback should be $9.99 or higher to capture the 60% royalty tier.
- Category strategy: one broad category with proven traffic (Children's eBooks > Animals) and one niche category (Children's eBooks > Growing Up & Facts of Life > Friendship & Social Skills).

**Social Media Platform Selection**
- Pinterest reaches 40% of U.S. parents and 52% of expecting parents. It functions as a visual search engine — pins continue driving traffic months or years after posting. It is the second-highest converting platform for book sales after Amazon itself. External Pinterest traffic now directly boosts Amazon rank. **Highest-leverage new platform.**
- Instagram engagement benchmarks (Q1 2026): carousel posts average 0.52%, Reels 0.50%, single images 0.35%. The strongest algorithmic signal is now "sends per reach." Nano-accounts (under 10K) average 5–6% engagement — the best bracket. Do not try to grow fast at the expense of quality content.
- TikTok: strong children's book influencer infrastructure, but requires consistent video creation. High ROI but high time cost. Defer until after Books 1 and 2 are ranked and earning.
- Facebook groups: 20-minute participation windows in 3–4 active parent groups. Contribute genuinely, mention the book only when relevant.

**Advertising**
- Amazon Sponsored Products ads for children's books: typical CPC $0.15–$0.75. One data point: $650/month in Amazon ads sold 220–300 children's books, roughly breaking even on ads while building organic ranking. The real value of early Amazon ads is rank, not direct ROI.
- Facebook/Instagram Ads: require audience testing and creative iteration. Not the right tool for launch week. Consider at Week 6+ after proof-of-concept sales and reviews.

## Recommendations

---

### SECTION 1: Positioning and Hook

**"A series that builds emotional intelligence — not anxiety."**

Support pillars:
1. No villains. No fear. Just three animal friends figuring out how to be good to each other and to the world.
2. Built for reading aloud together. Under 20 minutes per book.
3. A complete world — 8 books, one forest, characters your child will know by name.
4. Social-emotional learning woven in naturally, not as a lesson.

Use "social-emotional learning" (SEL) in KDP keywords but NOT in marketing copy — parents respond to outcomes, not jargon. Teachers and librarians respond to "SEL" directly — use it in school outreach.

---

### SECTION 2: Platform Strategy

**Add Pinterest. This is the single most important new platform move.**
Create a Pinterest business account. Create three boards:
- "Whisperwood Books" — all book and character content
- "Read Aloud Books for Ages 4–6" — curated list including comps, your books mixed in
- "Bedtime Stories for Kids" — broader parenting content that attracts parents who then discover your books

Pin 3–5 times per week. Use keywords in every pin title and description: "early reader series ages 4-6," "enchanted forest children's books," "read aloud bedtime books."

**Instagram: stay and intensify, but with a content calendar.**
@whisperwoodbook is your primary brand presence. The illustrations are your biggest upcoming content asset.

**TikTok: defer until after launch.**

**Facebook: low-effort participation only.** No page. Participate in 3–4 large parent communities from your phone.

**No Substack, no Twitter/X, no YouTube yet.**

---

### SECTION 3: Scheduling Tools — Mobile-Friendly Workflow

**Buffer** (free plan): 3 channels (Instagram + Pinterest + one more) with 10 scheduled posts per channel. The mobile app is genuinely good.

Workflow: Once per week (Sunday evening or Monday morning), batch-create 5–7 posts in Buffer. Upload the image, write the caption, add hashtags, set the schedule. The rest of the week you are only responding to comments.

Upgrade to $6/month Essentials plan when posting more than 10 per channel per month.

### SECTION 4: Day-by-Day and Week-by-Week Launch Schedule

*Assumes illustrations for Books 1 and 2 are complete or close. Adjust "Pre-launch" to start from when they are complete.*

**Strategy: Launch Books 1 and 2 simultaneously.** Launching two books at once lets Amazon surface the series, lets readers immediately continue, and gives you the series sales rank that a single book cannot achieve.

**Week 1, Days 1–2: KDP setup**

- Set launch price: $0.99 ebook, $9.99 paperback.
- Plan to enroll both books in KDP Select on publishing day.

**Week 1, Days 3–5: Website SEO**
- Update meta tags in index.html. Title tag: "Whisperwood Books | Enchanted Forest Read Aloud Series for Ages 4–6." Add 150–160 character meta description with keywords. Add Open Graph and Twitter Card tags.

**Week 1, Days 5–7: Pinterest setup**
- Create Pinterest business account linked to whisperwoodbooks.com.
- Claim website on Pinterest (verified checkmark + pin analytics).
- Create the three boards listed above.
- Pin 5 seeding pins from your website.

**Week 2, Days 1–3: ARC outreach**
- Identify 10–15 children's book Bookstagrammers. Search #kidlit, #picturebooks, #childrensbooks. Filter for accounts that regularly post children's book reviews with photography (5K–20K followers will often say yes).
- Send epub via BookFunnel (free plan). Include: launch date, series premise in 3 sentences, character descriptions.
- Also identify 3–5 children's librarians on Instagram.
- Message 10 accounts this week.

**Week 2, Days 4–7: Content creation batch**
Create 15–20 Instagram posts and schedule in Buffer:
- 3 character introduction posts (Vera, Pip, Sage)
- 2 "world facts" posts ("In Whisperwood, the bellflowers work like telephones…")
- 2 quote cards from Book 1
- 1 series overview carousel (all 8 book titles/covers previewed)
- 2 parent-resonance posts ("Why we made a series with no villains…")
- 2 countdown posts for launch week
- 3 posts about Book 1 and Book 2 conflict/lesson (no spoilers)
- 2 reader-engagement posts ("Which animal friend is your child most like — Vera, Pip, or Sage?")

Schedule at 1 post per day during launch week, then 4–5 posts per week after.

**Week 3, Days 1–3: Amazon Ads preparation**
Set up Sponsored Products campaigns (activate within minutes of going live):

Campaign 1: Automatic targeting (Amazon picks keywords). Budget: $3/day.
Campaign 2: Manual targeting. Budget: $5/day. Keywords:
- "children's books ages 4-6"
- "read aloud books for kids"
- "early reader animal books"
- "enchanted forest picture book"
- "emotionally intelligent books for children"
- Comp title ASINs: Princess in Black, Elephant and Piggie, Frog and Toad, Mercy Watson

Total daily budget cap: $8/day per book = $16/day combined (~$480/month). Run for 60 days, then cut non-converting campaigns.

**Week 3, Days 4–5: Email list setup**
- Set up free Mailchimp account.
- Create landing page offering a free "Whisperwood World Guide" (one-page PDF: character descriptions, forest map, world facts) as a sign-up incentive.

**Week 3, Days 6–7: Final checks**
- Confirm ARC recipients have files and know the launch date.
- Double-check KDP listings.
- Confirm Pinterest has at least 10 pins live.
- Launch week Instagram content scheduled in Buffer.
- Prepare launch day Mailchimp email.

**Choose Tuesday or Wednesday.** Monday launches get lost in weekend news. Friday launches lose the first week of sales rank to the weekend.

Same day:

- Send Mailchimp launch email.

### SECTION 5: Paid Advertising — Specific Guidance

**Amazon Ads: Yes. Start on launch day. Non-negotiable.**
Intent is already established — the person is on Amazon, already looking for a book.

Budget: $8/day ($480/month). If not feasible, $5/day ($150/month) — data will take longer.

Expected result: first month likely break even or slight loss. Real return is rank improvement, which drives organic sales at no cost.

**Facebook/Instagram Ads: Not yet. Week 6 at earliest.**
Wait until 15+ reviews. Without social proof, ads will not convert.

**BookBub Featured Deal: 6-month radar.**
Highly selective. Rarely features books with under 50 reviews.

**Promoted Pins on Pinterest: Week 4.**
Start at $5/day. Target "best books for 4-year-olds," "birthday gifts for kids." Lower competition than Amazon or Facebook for this category.

In the heart of Whisperwood — a forest where bellflowers carry messages and the ancient oak keeps everyone's secrets — Vera the fox, Pip the squirrel, and Sage the turtle face a problem that cannot be solved by being the fastest, the smallest, or the wisest alone.
