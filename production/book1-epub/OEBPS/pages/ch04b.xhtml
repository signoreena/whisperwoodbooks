She led him to the oldest boulder at the river’s edge. It was round and grey and covered in soft moss. It had been sitting in the same spot since before anyone in Whisperwood could remember.

Sage pressed her hand flat against it and closed her eyes. “Excuse me,” she said quietly. Pip watched her face. The river crept higher. A mouse family hurried past below, bundles on their backs, moving fast and quiet.

“…A tree,” said Sage. Pip leaned in. His nose was now very close to the rock. He wasn’t sure why. It just seemed like the right thing to do. “A tree? What tree?” Sage put one hand on his shoulder, gently. He went still. They waited. “…It fell,” said Sage.
