Illustration 8 — COMMUNITY IMAGE: Mushrooms Placed; Relay Thinning
Early evening at a path bend in Whisperwood, where the forest path curves by a small brook. Sage stands with one hand on the bark of a tree at the precise bend — her other hand pointing directly to the ground before her, certain, leading. Behind her, Pip carefully and precisely lowers a cluster of pale dome-shaped quietcap mushrooms to the spot she has indicated — both hands steady, expression focused and genuinely careful. Vera watches with a list in her hand, pen lowered, ears forward, expression clean and respectful — she is following Sage’s lead without hesitation. Two small apprentice owls arrange the cluster in the background. Above in the canopy, the relay chains of songbirds are thinning — fewer birds visible than earlier spreads, the overlapping arcs lighter, some birds already folding their wings. Lantern mushrooms beginning to glow as evening falls.
