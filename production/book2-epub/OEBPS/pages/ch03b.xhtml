Chapter 3
Vera Has a Plan

Vera had already noticed.

She had spent Wednesday evening at the Mapleaf tree. The veins were not dark — no flooding, no structural danger — but they pulsed in a way she had not seen before. Quick and irregular. As if the forest itself were a little agitated.

She had already started a list. It was long. It included: relay pattern (irregular), volume (increasing), coverage (whole forest, no gaps), affected parties (everyone, probably).

When Pip found her, she was adding another entry.

“I know,” she said, before he said anything.

“Hazel can’t sleep,” said Pip.

“I know. Probably others too.” She held up the list. “I’ve been working on it.”

Pip looked at the list. “That’s a lot of lines.”

“It’s a complicated problem.”

“What’s the plan?”

Vera put the list away. Her bag was already on her shoulder. “Schedule the birds,” she said. “They’re relaying all night because there’s no order to it. Each bird adds to the last. I’ll set up a rotation — northern canopy first, then eastern, then southern, then rest. Night silence hours built in.”

Pip’s tail flicked. “Will the birds listen?”

“They will if I explain it clearly,” said Vera.

She turned and walked toward the canopy.
