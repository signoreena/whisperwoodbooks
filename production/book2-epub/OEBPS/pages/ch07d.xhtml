By the time they reached the third location, the forest had quieted to its ordinary nighttime voice — a rustling, a settling, a single robin singing one clean note.

Pip stood in the path and listened.

“Oh,” he said softly.

It had been Pip who knew where every relay point was. Without his scouting, Sage could not have found what she was listening for.

From the bakery, a light came on. Hazel was starting her dough.

“She’s making rolls,” Pip whispered. “Sage. Rolls.”

Sage looked at the light in the bakery window.

“You always are,” said Sage.

They walked home, lantern mushrooms lighting the path. In the forked elm, a songbird folded its wings and went to sleep.
