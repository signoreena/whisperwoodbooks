Illustration 2
Early morning in Whisperwood. Pip crouches at the stone step of a round-doored bakery tucked into the base of a large tree — one foot still mid-landing, bushy tail raised, eyes wide with concern, a bellflower stem held in one paw. On the step sits Hazel the hedgehog baker, her apron loose and untied in her lap, visible rings of exhaustion under her eyes. She is looking up at the canopy above. The bakery’s shutters are closed; the flat stone counter to the right is bare — no rolls, no warmth. In the canopy above, four or five small songbirds are visible in mid-relay.
