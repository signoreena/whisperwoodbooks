Illustration 3
Morning on a forest path in Whisperwood. Vera walks at full decisive stride toward the canopy — satchel on her shoulder, a long handwritten list in her paw, amber eyes fixed on the path ahead. Her ears are fully forward; her chin is slightly lifted; she has already decided. Half a step behind her, Pip runs to keep up, tail streaming behind him, one foot barely touching the ground, expression caught between enthusiasm and the first flicker of uncertainty — he is carrying nothing, which for him is unusual. Songbirds fill the mid-canopy above, a dozen in relay. In the far background, the enormous Mapleaf tree stands with gold-green leaf-veins — calm, unhelpful to Vera’s diagnosis. Lantern mushrooms glow along the path’s edges.
