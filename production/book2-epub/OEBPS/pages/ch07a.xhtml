Illustration 7
A quietcap mushroom grove in Whisperwood. Pale, dome-shaped mushrooms grow in tight clusters across the forest floor, their matte surfaces slightly soft-edged — they absorb visual definition around them, making the air near each cluster fractionally quieter-seeming. Into this grove, a small group is led by Oswin — he moves matter-of-factly, wings slightly open. Behind him, Vera walks with purpose, a short list in her paw, coordinating. Pip carries two large armloads of mushrooms, slightly unstable, mouth open in concentration. At the back, Sage stands at the grove’s edge and has stopped — not her usual listening-stillness, but a different kind: release. Her hands are at her sides. Her expression is quieter than in any earlier spread. Two small apprentice owls and three raccoons work in the background. Rich late-afternoon forest light, slightly muffled in quality within the grove.
