Chapter 5
Pip Helps

Sage had not come to the library tree. Sage was still at home.

She had made it as far as her front step — one step out, then another — and sat with both hands pressed flat on the wooden boards. She could hear the relay in the canopy: same note, same note, always the same note, and underneath it the whole forest humming with it.

Her head ached. Her shell felt tight.

Pip found her there.

“I tried something,” he said, dropping onto the step beside her, a stick still in one paw.

“What did you try?”

Pip looked slightly less certain than usual. “I went to the relay point at the eastern fork — the loudest one — and I sang louder than all the birds. To kind of break it up.”

Sage looked at him.

“It worked for about four seconds,” said Pip. “And then every bird in the eastern section joined in to relay my voice as well. I think there is now a message going around the forest that says ‘LOUD NOISE AT EASTERN FORK.’” He set down the stick. “That is not helpful.”

Sage almost smiled.

“No,” she said. “But you found the loudest point.”

Pip looked at her. “…I did, didn’t I.”

Sage pressed her hands flat on the step again. She was listening.

“Pip,” she said slowly. “Can you take me to the relay points? All of them?”

“The canopy ones?”

“Yes.”

Pip hesitated. “It’s pretty loud up there.”

“I know,” said Sage.
