Wednesday evening in Whisperwood. The canopy is busy with songbirds — a dozen small birds visible across the upper third of the scene, each in mid-relay, beak open, wing extended, passing the same note forward. They look purposeful and slightly too numerous. The village of Whisperwood is mostly asleep below: tree-homes with dark windows, a path lit by clusters of small amber lantern mushrooms. One exception: a large old willow tree with a small warm-lit hollow partway up — the home of Sage. Her silhouette is visible against the window, hands pressed flat against the wood of the interior walls, listening.

Chapter 1
Something Is Wrong with the Birds

It started on a Wednesday.

The songbirds of Whisperwood had always carried the news. Everyone knew that. A songbird told you when the river ran fast. A songbird told you when the first frost was coming. A songbird told you when the hedgehog baker had warm rolls, which was useful and very popular.
