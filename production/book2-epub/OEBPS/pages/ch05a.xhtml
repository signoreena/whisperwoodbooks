Illustration 5
Outside the round-doored hollow of a willow tree in Whisperwood — a worn wooden step, willow fronds hanging to either side. Pip sits on the step beside Sage — both of them still, which for Pip is remarkable. A stick lies on the step beside him, clearly finished being useful. His tail is curled around himself; his expression is sheepish and wondering, eyes wide, watching Sage. Sage sits with both hands pressed flat on the wooden step-boards, not looking at Pip but forward and slightly down — her listening posture. The corners of her mouth are very slightly lifted. She is almost smiling. Through the willow fronds above, five or six songbirds are visible in mid-relay, carrying the noise onward. Dappled green-gold morning light through the willow canopy.
