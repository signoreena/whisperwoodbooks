Chapter 2
The Baker Cannot Bake

The hedgehog baker’s name was Hazel. She made her rolls before dawn, while the forest was still quiet. She had done this every morning for as long as anyone could remember.

On Thursday, there were no rolls.

Pip noticed first, which was unusual. But Pip had been up early, bouncing on a branch, and had passed the bakery at the usual hour and found the shutters closed.

He found Hazel sitting outside her door with her apron in her lap, not tied. She had rings under her eyes.

“Hazel?” said Pip. “No rolls?”

“No sleep,” said Hazel. “No sleep, no rolls. The birds.” She waved one small paw at the canopy. “All night. It doesn’t stop. It gets louder. And then it gets louder still.”

Pip looked up. He could hear it — a chatter, a relay, a swelling of overlapping voices, each one picking up the last note and handing it forward, always just a little faster.

He had not really noticed before.

“Hm,” said Pip.

He pulled out his bellflower and rang it, because there was clearly a problem and that meant it was time to find Vera.
