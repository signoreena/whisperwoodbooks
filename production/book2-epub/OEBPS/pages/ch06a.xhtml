Illustration 6 — KEY IMAGE: Sage at the Forked Elm
The most important image in the book. Sage stands at the fork of a large elm tree — the point where the trunk divides into two great branches — one hand pressed flat against the pale bark at the join, eyes closed, jaw set. Her patterned shell is visible from the side; both feet are planted; she has not moved. Above her in the canopy, three separate relay chains of songbirds converge at this exact elm: birds arriving from the upper left, upper right, and far center, each group of four or five in mid-relay, their arcing relay paths crossing and stacking in overlapping warm amber-orange spirals at the fork. The visual noise of the crossing chains fills the upper two-thirds of the image. Below the fork, one step back on the branch, Pip stands completely still, tail low, watching Sage with wide eyes and held breath — awe, not movement.

Chapter 6
What Sage Heard

They went to the relay points one by one.

At each one, Sage stood still and pressed one hand against the nearest tree. She did not cover her ears. She listened through the noise — past the volume, into the shape of it.

It was hard.

The first relay point made her stop on the path. The second made her close her eyes. At the third — the loudest, high in a forked elm above the owl’s library tree — she stood with her jaw set and her hand flat on the bark for a long time.
