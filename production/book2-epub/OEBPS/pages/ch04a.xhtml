Illustration 4 — KEY SPREAD: Library Tree Interior
Inside the library tree in Whisperwood — a vast hollow tree interior lined with curved shelves rising twenty feet, packed with scrolls, sealed boxes, and pressed objects between bark boards. Small amber lantern mushrooms glow in wall sconces at shelf-height; the upper shelves fade into shadow. An entrance at the far left shows a rectangle of forest daylight. At the center of this overwhelming space, a tiny very old owl (Oswin) faces Vera — he wears reading spectacles made of two curved pieces of bark, wings folded against his body, expression patient and immovable. Vera stands completely still, her fox ears moved back from their usual forward position — not flat, just back, the involuntary tell. She holds her long handwritten list folded in half in her paw; she has not put it away. Her mouth is closed. The scale of the library makes them both seem small, but Vera smaller.

Chapter 4
The Owls Say Not That Way

The owls lived in the library tree at the center of Whisperwood.

Vera went there first, because the owls tracked the songbird network and she needed data. The head owl was named Oswin. He was very old and had reading spectacles made of two curved pieces of bark. He was not surprised to see Vera.

“The relay problem,” said Vera. “I have a plan—”

“Yes,” said Oswin. “So did the last three creatures who came to me.”
