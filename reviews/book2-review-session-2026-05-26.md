## Consistency & Arc Findings

### Donna — Within-Book Prose & Plot

**D1 · Priority: HIGH**
**Location:** Chapter 1, paragraph 5
**Issue:** Timeline contradiction. "It started on a Wednesday" vs. "Something had been wrong all week." These directly contradict each other.
**Fix:** Replace "all week" with "since Wednesday." → **Route to Beatrice**

**D2 · Priority: MEDIUM**
**Location:** Chapter 3 / Chapter 4
**Issue:** Chapter 4 says "The Mapleaf had told her nothing useful" — but Chapter 3 never shows Vera actively querying it as a diagnostic tool. She only observes it passively. The Chapter 4 line implies a null query result that the reader hasn't seen.
**Fix:** Either add one sentence to Chapter 3 confirming Vera tried to read it diagnostically, or soften Chapter 4 to "The Mapleaf had shown her something wrong, but not why." → **Route to Beatrice**

**D3 · Priority: MEDIUM**
**Location:** Chapter 6 (mouse family appearance)
**Issue:** The mouse family appears at the elm with no prior introduction in Book 2. Their specificity (framed painting, notebook) reads as a callback, but for a standalone reader it is unearned. *(Also flagged by Florence — see F3.)*
**Fix:** Add one sentence in Ch 1 or Ch 2 establishing the mouse family lives near the forked elm, OR remove them from Ch 6. → **Route to Beatrice**

**D4 · Priority: MEDIUM**
**Location:** Chapter 5 / Chapter 4
**Issue:** Pip tries the eastern fork in Ch 5. In Ch 4, Oswin says the raccoons already tried the eastern fork and made things worse. Pip effectively repeats the location with no awareness of this.
**Fix:** Either move Pip to a different relay point, or have Sage note the parallel — which would deepen the "trying harder doesn't work" theme. → **Route to Beatrice**

**D5 · Priority: MEDIUM**
**Location:** Chapter 6
**Issue:** Sage identifies the western oak and the path bend by the brook as crossing nodes — but the text shows her reaching this conclusion at the elm. It is not made clear whether she deduced the other two from the elm or would need to visit them. The chapter structure describes three relay point visits, but only names the elm as the third.
**Fix:** Add one sentence at the elm making clear she can feel the other two chains originating from specific directions. → **Route to Beatrice**

**D6 · Priority: HIGH** *(confirmed by Vera arc, Pip arc, Harriett, Chloe)*
**Location:** Chapter 7, closing line
**Issue:** "You always are" has no clear grammatical antecedent. Multiple reviewers and three focus group children could not parse it.
**Fix:** Pip arc proposes adding "I'll be there first thing," said Pip. before Sage's line — restoring the missing antecedent and making the closer work. → **Route to Beatrice**

**D7 · Priority: LOW**
**Location:** Chapter 7 structure
**Issue:** The final three steps of the Whisperwood Pattern (Community Gathers / Resolution / Reflection) are all compressed into Chapter 7 at ~210 words. The community-gathering payoff feels slightly rushed.
**Fix:** Either expand Chapter 7 to give each location one brief sentence as the quiet spreads, or split into separate chapters. → **Route to Beatrice (lower priority)**

### Florence — World Consistency

**F1 · Priority: HIGH**
**Location:** Chapter 4, Oswin scene
**Issue:** The owl rule — owls answer, they do not brief — is partially violated. Oswin volunteers the history of failed attempts and the key loop insight before Vera asks a specific question. Vera's only question ("What have the others tried?") comes mid-scene and covers the history; the conceptual insight about the loop is largely volunteered.
**Fix:** Oswin's opening response should be a question back at Vera ("What did you plan to do?") rather than pre-emptive disclosure of the full picture. The loop insight should be delivered only in response to a question Vera earns. → **Route to Beatrice**

**F2 (verified clean):** Lantern mushroom terminology — correct throughout.

**F3 · Priority: MEDIUM** *(overlaps D3)*
**Location:** Chapter 6, mouse family description
**Issue:** "At the base of the elm, the mouse family sat in their temporary shelter — Pa very calm, Ma holding the framed painting, Big Daughter's notebook open, Baby asleep." Four named members with four specific details violates the bible's one-line background rule. Middle Son is also absent.
**Fix:** Reduce to "At the base of the elm, the mouse family sat in their temporary shelter." All detail (Pa, Ma, Big Daughter, Baby, props) moves to illustrator notes. → **Route to Beatrice**

**F4 (verified clean):** Bellflower usage — Pip's carried bellflower is not a violation, but a canonical question is noted: are bellflowers fixed infrastructure (Book 1) or personal portable devices (Book 2)? Flag for writers to decide before Books 3–4.

**F5 (verified clean):** Raccoons at eastern fork — their attempted physical intervention is correct role usage. No violation.

**Notes for future books:**
- Bellflower portability needs a canonical answer before Books 3–4
- Owl question discipline — each future Oswin scene should be audited: is the character asking, or is Oswin monologuing?
- Mouse family one-line rule — re-read before every book draft
- Middle Son — his visual signature (fascinated by nearby events) makes the loudest spot in the forest during a noise crisis a natural illustration opportunity

### Sage Arc Review

**S1 · Priority: HIGH**
**Location:** Chapter 5, threshold decision
**Issue:** Sage's decision to walk into the noise — the emotional climax of her arc in Book 2 — happens entirely offscreen. We get: she is frozen on the step → she asks Pip to take her → they are already moving. The moment of choosing to go in despite the cost is not dramatized.
**Fix:** Add 3–5 sentences at the close of Chapter 5, between "I know" and the chapter end, showing Sage's threshold moment as visible physical action (standing up, hands on the doorframe, something the reader sees). → **Route to Beatrice**

**S2 · Priority: HIGH** *(confirmed by Harriett)*
**Location:** Chapter 7, closing beats
**Issue:** Sage's contribution — staying in the loudest noise in the forest to find what no one else could feel — is never spoken aloud by any character. Pip's contribution is narrated. Vera's apology names her own failure. Sage's act is absent from the spoken world of the book.
**Fix:** Add one line of dialogue (Pip or Vera) naming what Sage did. Does not need to be long. Harriett's Option B: Pip names Sage's act and Sage deflects to the rolls — resolves both S2 and the "You always are" ambiguity in one edit. → **Route to Beatrice**

**S3 · Priority: LOW**
**Location:** Chapter 7, Vera's acknowledgment
**Issue:** Vera's "You stayed in the noise to find where it crossed. I should have asked you first." explains Sage's contribution in speech rather than through action. Slightly over-explicit for the series register; risks telling the moral rather than showing it.
**Fix:** Consider trimming to just "You didn't know what I could hear. / I didn't ask." and letting Oswin's reaction (a look, a silence) carry the weight the first sentence currently carries. → **Route to Beatrice (low priority)**

**S4 · Priority: MEDIUM** → **Route to Emily (bible change)**
**Location:** Series bible, Book 2 outline
**Issue:** The bible describes "Sage negotiating with the songbirds to relay news in quieter shifts" as part of the Book 2 solution. The finished book replaces this entirely with the quietcap mushroom / intersection-node solution, which is strictly better (more consistent with Sage's gift, more consistent with the world's physics, makes Sage's overwhelm narratively necessary). The bible must be updated to match the completed book.
**Fix:** Update bible Book 2 outline to: three relay intersection nodes, quietcap mushrooms at forked elm / western oak / path bend by brook; Sage's physical-diagnostic method; Oswin's role in locating the quietcap grove. Remove all "negotiating with songbirds" framing. → **Route to Emily**
